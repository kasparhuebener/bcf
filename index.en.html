Contemporary circus – What is it?

„Contemporary Circus considers itself as a staged artistic art form. The dominant format is the theatre performance based on a dramaturgical and aesthetic concept. Circus elements and techniques become the performer’s means of expressions whose personality, biography and physicality have a major impact on their work.

„A little, chubby ropedancer can cause much more sensation than a more physical and athletic one that shows equal incomparable talent.“
- Georg Daxner, Winterfest Salzburg

The main focus is not the greatest physical achievement but the sublime artistic performance. Contemporary circus often unites multiple arts such as acting, dance, music, visual arts, new media, etc. The mixing of genres - the transcending of boarders – is a natural opportunity for many artists to inspire people through their work and receive inspiration from others.”

For more info, please see our partner’s website Initiative Neuer Zirkus .

Cirk La Putyka (CZ) - Slapstick Sonata

The nightshift of a dark warehouse in Prague prepares for another tedious night. But nothings seems as it is. Witness how the labourers break out of their mundane routine and transform the night into a vibrant and glorious explosion of colours, humour, movement and silliness of physical theatre. Slapstick Sonata is a beautiful blending of visual poetry, physical comedy and contemporary circus somewhere between absurd and surreal.

Cirk La Putyka is an ensemble dedicated professionally to the “Contemporary Circus”, trying to transcend the lines between acrobatics, contemporary dance, puppetry, concert and sport. Since 2009, the artistic director Rosta Novak has created seven productions that have performed with great success at international festivals like EXPO – Shanghai, Sydney festival, Edinburgh - Fringe festival, Biennale du cirque de Marseilles and many more.

Val & Coline - Championnat

Welcome to the 12th international dance marathon!
For 4 months, 3 weeks and almost 78 hours, the two finalists Coline and Valentin compete for their victory. They aim not only for the first place, they want more. In 100 beeps, Coline and Valentin will have set the new world record. Every 12 seconds sounds a beep, the signal to change something: an acrobatic element, a sentence, a song, something essentially new!

Coline and Valentin are a professional hand-to-hand duo. Though, their work has nothing in common with a traditional circus act. Reaching the climax in total exhaustion, their first piece tackles the quest for the new. It is a battle for invention and creativity. Choreographed by Simon Tanguy, a rising star of contemporary dance in France, the two graduates from the French National Circus school present the madness of their creative idea in 100 beeps.

Darragh McLoughlin (IRE) – The Whistle
© Michele Vindimian

"When you hear the sound of the whistle you have to close your eyes. When you hear it again you may open them."
 What if you were at the same time spectator and creator of a piece? 
A juggler, a whistle and the audience together create a multitude of moments: “Time travel - Déjà vu – Glitches - Fast forward – Rewind - Déjà vu
All without technics or special effects. 
A journey through several different hilarious yet often poetic narratives.
Close your eyes!  Open them! 
Will you play the game or will you cheat? 
Either way you will be played!"

Darragh McLoughlin is a contemporary circus performer/creator from Ireland. He founded Squarehead Productions in 2011 with the aim of bringing together different contemporary artistic practices. After completing a foundations course in the Jonglier Katakomben in Berlin, he graduated in 2012 with a bachelor degree from the Academy for Circus and Performance Art (ACAPA) in the Netherlands. Since then Darragh has been in a perpetual state of creation for this two shows "Fragments of a Mind", and "The Whistle".

squareheadproductions.com

Natalie Reckert (DE) - ~image~

„A handstand, that's a pulsing structure, formed by a had, a body, two arms and legs. Every single part is being hold together by Proteins, that will melt at 40°. That's why you can take a handstand apart, without damaging any parts.“
In ~image~, handstands are the center of a small universe, in which Natalie is a machine: invincible, complex and virtuosic. But then again, she remains human: fragile, evancescent, made from icing – melting at 20°.

Natalie Reckert does handstands since she was only 5 years. Since then she perfectionned her abilities at the „National Centre for Circus Arts“ in London and at the „Vision in Motion“- school for contemporary dance in Kassel. The public at the prestigious GOP Varietes had the chance to admire her as a handstandartist. She's been working with the „Generating Company“, „Sugar Beast Circus“ and Ilona Jäntti. She lives and works in Kassel and Berlin. ~image~ is her first own production.

nataliereckert.com

Tanter (NO, SWE, DEN) -
Vixen - not to fall under the pressure of improvised movements
© Einar Kling Odencrants
© Andreas Bergmann Steen

Absurd, insane and magnificent: Tanter proudly presents: VIXEN. With two ropes, contortion and a swinging trapeze we are working with the contrast between the elegant and the hysterical, the brutal and the self-controlled. In this spirit we promise you an hour of full on fanatically female soul flashing explosions.

Tanter, the company around Moa, Karoline and Elise, started this project in april 2013, after finishing their studies in France and Stockholm. During the creation they focused on the female role, countering expectations and impulses, working with emotions and instincts. Renowned french-canadian artist Angela Laurier helped as an outside eye, while Anika Barkan, as the instructor, put all of it together in mysterious ways.
